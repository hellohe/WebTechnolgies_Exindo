South Sumatra

Pempek Palembang

Pempek (also called mpek-mpek or empek-empek) is a savoury fishcake delicacy from Palembang, Indonesia. it is made of fish and tapioca. Pempek is served with yellow noodles and a dark, rich sweet and sour sauce called kuah cuka(vinegar sauce).

Tempoyak

Tempoyak is a dish which originated from fermented durian. Tempoyak is usually consumed as a side dish of rice. Tempoyak can also be eaten directly but is rarely done, because the acidity and aroma of tempoyak itself. Tempoyak can also used to be some ingredient for cooking.

Pindang Ikan Patin

Pindang ikan is a soupy dish known as a culinary icon of South Sumatra. Almost all pindang is cooked with fish, of which the most popular kinds are patin fish (silver catfish) and baung fish (Mystus nigriceps). Those who prefer a thick sauce do so with the use of tempoyak (fermented durian).

Mie Celor

Mie Celor is a noodle dish served in coconut milk soup, ebi (dried shrimp) broth, boiled egg, and sprinkled with sliced fresh celery, scallion, and fried shallot. The dish is originated from Palembang, South Sumatra, Indonesia, and together with Pempek it has become Palembang specialty. It is made from rather large yellow noodle, with the size similar of Japanese buckwheat noodle.
