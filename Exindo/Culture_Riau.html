Riau

Zapin (Traditional Dance)

Zapin Dance is a Malay dance which is imbued with religious and educational messages. The dance has its roots in the Malay culture and traditions of Riau that hold diverse values in their lives. This traditional dance is highlighted by its beautiful diverse movements originally born out of the skillful movements of the feet as originally performed by Arabic and Persian males. The Zapin Dance in Indonesia spread together with the spread of Islam that was brought by Arabic traders from the Hadramaut. In Arabic, Zapin is known as al raqh wal zafn.

Melemang (Traditional Dance)

Melemang dance is said to have existed since the time of the kingdom Bentan. This means that the dance has been known since the 12th century. That said, at that time, melemang not include consumption of folk dance, but dance palace. The dancers were not ordinary people, but the ladies who come from around the palace, including the so-called Tanjungpisau acacia. This dance is presented when the King was resting. Any staging of the dancers demonstrate their skills by taking something (handkerchiefs, coins, etc.) by melemang (standing membongkokkan body toward the rear). Therefore, this dance is called melemang.

In Tanjungpisau dance is better known as Dance Melemang Melemang acacia or shittim Bintan. In accordance with the purpose which is nothing but entertain the king, the art which combines elements of dance, music and singing tells about the life of a king in a kingdom. Therefore, there is a role as king, empress, princess, lady's maid and so forth.
