Banten

Emping

Emping is the name of a type of Indonesian chips, a bite-size snack kripik cracker made of melinjo or belinjo (gnetum gnemon) nut. The emping crackers have a slightly bitter taste.[1] The emping snack available in the market can be plain (original), salty or sweet depends on the addition of salt or caramelized sugar.

Sate Bandeng

A unique delicacy from Banten. It is a satay made from boneless "Bandeng" (milkfish). The seasoned spicy milkfish meat is separated from the small bones, then placed back into the milkfish skin, clipped by a bamboo stick, and grilled over charcoal.

Taoge Goreng

Fried bean sprouts are the typical dishes of Banten. This food is rarely found today, perhaps only a few traders are still selling typical food of the Bogor. Beginning called fried bean sprouts, as a way of cooking the sprouts such as fried noodles, boiled and marinade separated togenya fused with oil.

Currently fried sprouts mixed with the modern way of cooking already, different with the former, which means cooking still use firewood, trays of copper, and a sauce that named pendil (derived from soil).
